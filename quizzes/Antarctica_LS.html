Quick Learning Tips – Antarctica

📍 Location

  • Located around the South Pole.
  • Surrounded by the Indian, Atlantic, and Pacific Oceans – collectively the Southern Ocean.

📏 Size and Ice Coverage

  • Area: 14.2 million sq. km (5.5 million sq. miles).
  • 98% covered by thick ice.

🌍 Division of the Continent

  • East Antarctica: High ice-covered plateau (Greater Antarctica).
  • West Antarctica: Ice sheet over mountain islands (Lesser Antarctica).
  • Separated by the Transantarctic Mountain Chain.

❄️ Climate

  • Average winter: −34.4°C (−30°F).
  • Lowest temp: −89.4°C (−129°F).
  • Highest temp: 15°C (59°F).
  • Holds 90% of world’s ice and 80% of its freshwater.

🕐 Seasons

  • 2 seasons: Summer & Winter.
  • 6 months of daylight in summer, darkness in winter.

🌿 Vegetation

  • No trees or bushes.
  • Only lichens, mosses, and algae survive.

🐧 Wildlife

  • Marine animals: Fish, whales, seals.
  • Top predator: Leopard seal.
  • Familiar animal: Penguin.

🐋 Featured Animals

  • Blue-eye Trevally
  • Fin Whale
  • Humpback Whale
  • Antarctic Minke Whale
  • Leopard Seal
  • Penguin

📚 Interesting Facts

  • Oldest ice: 800,000+ years.
  • No official time zone – stations follow home country time.

🏠 Life on Antarctica

  • No permanent residents.
  • 1,000 in winter, 5,000 in summer (scientists/support staff).
  • Tourists visit during summer via cruise ships.

🌊 Ice Decline & Global Impact

  • Ice melting rapidly, raising sea levels.
  • 2023 saw record-low sea ice – causes heat loss, storms.
  • Impacts penguins, seals, and global climate systems.
  • Southern Ocean helps store carbon and move heat globally.
  • Low ice cover leads to extreme winters and climate disruption.
