Section 1.A: Unseen Comprehension (Non-Fiction)

The Amazon rainforest, often called the "Lungs of the Planet," is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, covering an area of over 5.5 million square kilometers. It spans across nine countries in South America, with the majority lying within Brazil. This incredible ecosystem is home to an estimated 10% of the world's known species, including around 40,000 plant species, 3,000 freshwater fish species, and more than 370 types of reptiles.

The Amazon River, the lifeblood of the rainforest, is the second-longest river in the world and the largest by water discharge. It flows for approximately 6,400 kilometers before emptying into the Atlantic Ocean. The sheer biodiversity of the Amazon is astounding, with new species still being discovered regularly. Indigenous communities have lived in harmony with the rainforest for thousands of years, possessing vast traditional knowledge about its flora and fauna.

However, this vital ecosystem is under threat. Deforestation, primarily for cattle ranching, agriculture (like soy and palm oil), logging, and mining, is a major concern. Scientists warn that continued deforestation could lead to irreversible damage, impacting global climate patterns and leading to a significant loss of biodiversity. Conservation efforts are underway, but the challenge is immense, requiring global cooperation and sustainable practices.

Section 2.A: Unseen Comprehension (Fiction)

It had been empty for years, and the townsfolk whispered stories about it – tales of flickering lights, strange noises, and a mysterious old woman who had once lived there. Maya, a curious and adventurous girl of twelve, had always been fascinated by the house. One crisp autumn afternoon, armed with a flashlight and a sense of daring, she decided to explore it.

The rusty gates creaked open with a groan that seemed to echo through the silent, overgrown garden. As Maya tiptoed up the crumbling stone path, the wind whistled through the broken window panes, sounding like a mournful sigh. The front door was ajar, a dark, inviting slit. Taking a deep breath, she pushed it open.

Inside, dust lay thick on everything, like a soft grey blanket. Cobwebs draped the furniture, and the air was heavy with the scent of decay and forgotten things. A grand staircase spiralled upwards into the gloom. From somewhere deep within the house, Maya thought she heard a faint, tinkling sound, like a distant wind chime. Her heart hammered against her ribs, a mixture of fear and excitement.
