Australia Geography – Key Learning Points

  • The Nullarbor Plain is a vast, flat, treeless limestone plateau in southern Australia.
  • ‘Nullarbor’ means ‘no tree’ in Latin.
  • The Nullarbor Plain slopes gently towards the south.
  • It lies to the north of the Great Australian Bight.
  • The Nullarbor Plain features the world’s longest straight stretch of railway.
  • Water flowing through limestone has formed deep underground caverns in the Nullarbor Plain.
  • Nullarbor Cave is a prime example of such limestone formations.
  • The Nullarbor Plain forms steep cliffs along the Great Australian Bight.
  • The coastline here is described as a ‘tight bay’.
  • Stalactites and stalagmites are formed in limestone caves.
  • Stalactites form from calcite deposited by dripping water on cave roofs.
  • Stalagmites rise from the cave floor and often look like heaps of molten wax.
  • When stalactites and stalagmites meet, they form pillars or columns.
  • Stalactites hang from the ceiling; stalagmites grow up from the ground.
  • Calcite is the mineral responsible for forming these cave features.
  • Uluru (Ayers Rock) is a large sandstone rock formation in central Australia.
  • Uluru is sacred to the Anangu people, the traditional owners.
  • The Great Artesian Basin is the largest and deepest underground water reservoir in Australia.
